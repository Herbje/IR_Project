How Long Is a Moment? In modern English, the term moment refers to an unspecific short amount of time. The term is thought to date to the 14th century and was used to refer to a period of 90 seconds. An hour in medieval times therefore consisted of 40 moments. The Hebrew calendar has a shorter definition of a moment, referred to as rega, which is roughly equivalent to 5/144 of a second.
